    Abstract

  • The Integrated Learning (IL) program has provided an opportunity for grade IX students to experience direct entrepreneurship by organizing a bazaar. In this program, 6 students are grouped, giving them the opportunity to use their creative minds to make their personalized store, and handmade products that were sold to customers during the bazaar. Our group focused on developing products through various creative processes, including biotechnology, sewing, and crafting. This project required us to not only create quality products, but also to consider the financial and marketing aspect and view of running a business. By participating in this program, valuable insights were gained into how businesses operate, from the execution of making the products to selling them real-time.
  • At the start of the project, brainstorming discussions were held to create innovative ideas from each group member, sometimes online and another period of time offline. During this time, different product options were discussed, and examinations or trials of making that product were conducted to decide which items would be ideal to produce. Once the final products had been decided upon, materials had been bought and gathered, it was time to engage our production plan. Each member had been assigned specific tasks based on their strengths, making sure that every aspect of the business was handled effectively. Additionally, marketing strategies were developed, and promotional materials were designed to attract customers during the bazaar.
  • Throughout the production process, there were a few adjustments that had to be made as a few challenges were encountered during the process. One of the biggest difficulties was managing time, as the production process took longer than expected. Some materials were unavailable/out of stock in online shops, requiring us to find substitutes while maintaining product quality. Pricing decisions were also difficult, as we had to balance affordability with making a profit. Through teamwork and effective communication, these obstacles were overcome, and the bazaar was successfully executed, providing us with real life experience in entrepreneurship.
  • Through this project, many lessons were learned about business management, creativity, and collaboration. It became much more clear that running a business is hard, and requires not only skill but also adaptability and problem-solving abilities. Mistakes were made, but they were remembered as opportunities for growing our capabilities. Teamwork and bond was strengthened, responsibilities were shared. This also teaches us the importance of relying on one another. Finally, we learned to appreciate the hard work of entrepreneurship and the importance of good and structured planning.
